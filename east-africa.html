Explore East African Cuisine

East Africa

East African cuisine is known for its rich variety of flavors influenced by a mix of indigenous, Arabic, Indian, and European cultures. The food often features spices like cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon, and the staple ingredients include rice, maize, beans, and root vegetables like sweet potatoes and cassava. Popular cooking methods include grilling, roasting, and steaming, often using banana leaves or palm oil in the preparation.

Signature Dishes

Ugali

Ugali is a popular dish in East Africa, made from maize flour and water, and served as a side to a variety of stews and vegetable dishes. It is a key staple in Kenya, Tanzania, and surrounding countries.

Nyama Choma

Nyama Choma, or roasted meat, is one of the most beloved dishes in East Africa. It typically consists of beef, goat, or chicken marinated in spices and grilled over an open flame.

Sambusa

A deep-fried pastry filled with spiced meat, lentils, or vegetables. This snack is popular in Kenya, Ethiopia, and Somalia, and is often enjoyed as an appetizer or street food.
